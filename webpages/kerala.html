Sabarimala

Sabarimala is a temple town on the bank of the River Pampa.Named after the mythological character, Sabarimala shelters the famous Ayyapa temple. The temple is also known as Dharma Sashta and is believed to be the son of Shiva and Mohini, the feminine incarnation of Lord Vishnu. People believe that Vishnu's incarnate, Parasurama, placed the Ayyappa Idol at the top of the mountain.

Periyar National Park

Periyar National Park & Wildlife Sanctuary, locating in Thekkady is the perfect example of nature's bounty with great scenic beauty and rich biodiversity. In this astounding location of Kerala, the Periyar National Park is considered as the most protected area for the elephants and the tigers. One of the most renowned national parks in South India, the Periyar National Park is set high at the Western Ghat ranges.

Beemapally Mosque

It is a very important place of worship for all the followers of Islam. Beema Beevi and her son are the reason for the immense popularity of this mosque. A festival called Beemapalli Uruz is very famous and devotees from not just islam, but other religions also come to become a part of this. The main attraction of the mosque is tomb of Beema Beevi. This is one of the most beautiful mosques in Kerala.

Santa Cruz Basilica

One of the most famous churches in Kerala and one among the 8 Basilicas, Santa Cruz Basilica is a beautiful church located in Fort Kochi. Known for spectacular Indo-European and Gothic architecture, historical paintings, murals and frescos, this church is more than 500 years old and was built during the arrival of Portugese in 1500 CE.
